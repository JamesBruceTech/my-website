About Me

From a young age, I was fascinated by how systems worked—how devices communicated, how software operated behind the scenes, and how everything came together to make things function smoothly. This curiosity led me to explore and experiment, whether it was fixing issues or optimising systems.

My earliest experience came as the unofficial family tech support: fixing Wi-Fi issues, setting up devices, and solving software problems. These moments taught me that effective IT support combines technical ability with empathy, patience, and clarity. This hands-on mindset has followed me through my education and professional career.

In the classroom, I often helped teachers troubleshoot projectors or fix broken whiteboards. Outside school, I experimented with hardware, BIOS tuning, dual-booting Linux, and building PCs. Over time, this evolved into a deeper understanding of system architecture, performance tuning, and IT support workflows.
